Defenses Against Social Engineering

1. Establish Safeguards

Implement standard trust frameworks for employees to reduce the risk of compromise by social engineering. Identify high-value targets in your environment, and then establish security protocols, policies, and procedures for the appropriate control and handling of sensitive information on those systems.

  • Conduct training, awareness, and test exercises within your organization.
  • Iterative threat modeling to review and improve related security controls.

2. Implement “Slow Thinking”

Develop solutions that force your employees to slow down and think in logical terms, thereby avoiding the cognitive biases and shortcuts social engineers most often exploit.

  • Configuring your phone-switching system to require extra verification steps.
  • Configuring your mail client to require additional steps before opening external email attachments.
  • Requiring users visiting non-whitelisted URLs to perform verification tasks.

Recommended Security Controls and Mitigations

Implement safeguards and controls from the NIST 800-53 standard to protect information and systems from social engineering attacks.

  • AC-3: Access Enforcement | (9) Controlled Release
  • PE-3: Physical Access Control | (2) Facility/Information System Boundaries
  • SC-7: Boundary Protection
  • AC-4: Information Flow Enforcement
  • PL-8: Information Security Architecture
  • SC-8: Transmission Confidentiality and Integrity
  • MA-4: Nonlocal Maintenance
  • MP-5: Media Transport | (1) Protection Outside Controlled Areas
