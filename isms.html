Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish an Information Security Management System (ISMS) at MJ Residence. I have established an ISMS in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001 (“ISO 27001”) that governs the processes required to protect home and information assets. This document specifies the scope of the ISMS and PIMS at MJ Residence.

Policy Scope

The policy applies to all home software and hardware infrastructure, devices, systems, and data, covers all home products and services, and must be adhered to by all residents and contractors.

Ownership

I, Mohammed Jangeer, am responsible for implementing and maintaining this policy.

Policy Objective

The objective of my Executive Management is to continually strengthen and improve the overall capabilities of the ISMS, to increase professional skills in terms of information security management and technology, to make the MJ Residence management system so complete that reliable certification standards are effective, to ensure information-related operations continue to meet certification standards, and to establish a sustainable operation plan that is cost-effective. Specific objectives include:

  • Information is only accessible to authorized persons.
  • Confidentiality of information is maintained.
  • Integrity of information is maintained throughout the process.
  • Business continuity plans are established, maintained, and tested.
  • All residents are trained on information security and compliance with the policy is mandatory.
  • All breaches of information security and suspected weaknesses are reported and investigated.
  • Procedures exist to support the policy, including virus control measures, passwords, and continuity plans.
  • Business requirements for the availability of information and systems will be met.
  • I am responsible for maintaining the policy and providing support and advice during its implementation.
  • All managers are directly responsible for implementing the policy and ensuring resident compliance in their respective areas.

Scope of the ISMS

The scope of the ISO 27001 certification is limited to the ISMS supporting the assets, technologies, and processes employed by MJ Residence located at [Residence Location]. This includes the protection of the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

Assets

The assets of the ISMS include information, application, database, cloud services, and personnel:

  • Home systems
  • Locations
  • Home locations
  • Departments

The following areas are in-scope for the ISMS:

  • Home areas
  • Personnel
  • All residents and associated personnel are in-scope for the ISMS.

Scope Exclusions and Boundaries

MJ Residence uses Amazon Web Services (“AWS”) for cloud computing and storage services. Amazon is responsible for managing logical access to the underlying network, system management, and storage devices for its cloud service hosting MJ Residence data.

Amazon is responsible for the physical security and environmental controls at data center facilities hosting MJ Residence systems; therefore, physical security and environmental controls are out of scope for those facilities.

Amazon is responsible for the secure deletion of data and disposal of equipment used to support MJ Residence operations.

MJ Residence information, application, database, facility, and personnel assets that are not related to the identified in-scope components are out of scope for the ISMS.

Information Security Objectives

Information security objectives are set and monitored annually by my ISMS Committee based on a clear understanding of requirements. The current information security objectives are as follows:

  • Protect the confidentiality, availability, and integrity of home, resident, and personal data.
  • Comply with applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations.
  • Achieve and maintain ISO 27001 certifications.

The action plans to achieve these objectives are maintained and reviewed annually by the ISMS Committee.

Risk Assessment and Risk Treatment

I have defined a process for identifying, analyzing, treating, and monitoring risks. The risk acceptance criteria and analysis are documented in the risk register and risk management policy.

Statement of Applicability (SOA)

The SOA defines information security control objectives and controls based on business needs, legal and regulatory requirements, and contractual obligations. It is reviewed and approved by management.

Performing Risk Assessments

Risk assessments are performed quarterly or as needed under various circumstances, such as significant changes, software development, third-party supplier engagements, and external changes.

Management Approval

The ISMS Committee reviews and approves the SOA and risk assessment highlights. Feedback may be provided during annual management reviews or as needed.

Resources and Competence

Annually, I assess personnel performance and competence against ISMS requirements, addressing any competency gaps through training, mentoring, reassignment, or supplementing with contractors.

Training and Awareness

All residents must complete Security Awareness Training upon joining and annually thereafter. Additional training may be required due to security incidents, new technology adoption, or policy changes. Records of training completion are maintained.

ISMS Communication

Internal communications relevant to the ISMS, such as updates, audit results, and continual improvement efforts, are shared with the Board of Directors, senior management, and all residents. External communications are shared on the company's website and with approved third parties as needed.

Document Approvals

Documentation is updated and approved annually, with the most recent version made available to all residents. A historical approval log tracks changes.

Documentation Archival, Retention, and Disposal

The ISMS Committee approves ISMS documents. The Information Security Management Leader implements directives and maintains policy changes. Documentation is retained for at least six years, with archived documents maintained for at least three years. Secure deletion is used for disposal.

ISMS Internal Audit (IA)

The internal audit evaluates the ISMS's compliance with ISO 27001, identifying opportunities for improvement. The scope includes all in-scope information assets and controls identified within the SOA. A third-party vendor may be engaged for internal audits.

Auditor Selection

Auditors must be trained and qualified, with proper segregation of duties. Auditor competence is validated based on education and experience.

Resources

Third-party auditors, along with the Information Security Management Leader and IS Team, conduct the audit.

Frequency & Schedule

Internal audits are conducted annually, with frequency adjusted based on audit findings and control effectiveness.

Audit Criteria

Audit criteria include ISMS policies and procedures, regulatory requirements, ISO 27001, and additional standards as necessary.

Audit Documentation

Auditors collect artifacts, documents, observations, and interviews as evidence. A final report is shared with the Information Security Leader and ISMS Committee.

Audit Records Retention

Audit evidence and documentation are protected and retained according to ISMS Documentation requirements.

Management Review

My ISMS Management Review Procedure ensures the ISMS operates effectively and continually improves. Inputs may include changes in context, needs of interested parties, legal requirements, risk assessment results, audit observations, and more. The ISMS Committee reviews and approves decisions and actions.

Corrective Action and Continual Improvement

Non-conformities identified during audits or reviews are addressed through a corrective action and improvement plan. This includes updating relevant documentation and monitoring the status of open items.

Policy Violation

All personnel must maintain the security, confidentiality, availability, integrity, and privacy of MJ Residence assets. Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination and legal prosecution.
